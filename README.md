### **1. Selected SDG and Reason for Selection**

**SDG Chosen:** **SDG 6 – Clean Water and Sanitation**

**Reason:**  
Access to clean and safe drinking water is a fundamental human right. In India, especially in Mumbai and Maharashtra, water contamination is a major issue due to pollution, poor infrastructure, and flooding. Contaminated water leads to millions of cases of waterborne diseases every year. This project leverages AI to provide an affordable, fast, and accessible solution for water quality assessment.

---

### **2. Problem Statement**

**What is the problem?**  
Many households, slums, and rural areas do not have access to quick and affordable water quality testing facilities. People often consume unsafe water unknowingly, resulting in serious health problems like diarrhea, cholera, typhoid, and other waterborne diseases.

**Who is affected?**  
- Residents of urban slums and rural areas  
- Low-income families  
- Local municipal authorities with limited monitoring resources

**Why is it serious?**  
According to WHO, contaminated water causes over 485,000 diarrheal deaths each year globally. In India, this number is significantly high. Without real-time monitoring tools, prevention remains difficult.

---

### **3. Project Objective**

To develop an AI-based intelligent system that can accurately predict whether water is potable (safe to drink) based on key chemical and physical parameters, thereby helping individuals and authorities make informed decisions.

---

### **4. Proposed Solution**

**AquaGuard** is a web-based AI application that allows users to:

- Input water quality parameters through an interactive interface
- Get instant prediction whether the water is **Safe** or **Unsafe**
- View prediction confidence score
- Upload water sample images (future-ready for advanced analysis)
- Access a visual dashboard

**How it Works:**  
The system uses a **Random Forest Machine Learning model** trained on a real water quality dataset to classify water as potable or non-potable.
